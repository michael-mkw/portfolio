About Me

Michael Wong

GIS Analyst  |  Surveyor

Background

I am a graduate of the Master of Geographic Information Systems at the University of Calgary in February 2024, with a bachelor's degree in geomatics and previous experience as a surveyor since 2014. A GIS and programming passionate, a travel holic, and a home cook.

My story

My first journey with GIS started during my time as a geomatics undergraduate student - I joined the volunteering community service projects in 2012 and 2013 organized by the university to remote hamlets in Jogjakarta, Indonesia and collaborated with the villagers in identifying local socio-economic issues, conducting geospatial analysis and presenting data cartographically.

What inspired me?

Later when I completed my geomatics degree and was working as a surveyor in the geomatics industry, I was always fascinated by the powerful GIS software and tools and their potential to facilitate spatial data management in the workplace. Bearing a surveyor's mindset in understanding the geospatial world and a recognition of the potential of GIS, I enrolled in the GIS graduate program - Master of Geographic Information Systems (MGIS) at the University of Calgary in 2022.

Glad to join the GIS industry

After completing the MGIS, I joined the Geospatial Centre of the Alberta Biodiversity Monitoring Institute (ABMI) in the position of GIS Technician from March to June 2024. I am now working as a Summer Research Assistant at the Boreal Ecosystem Recovery and Assessment (BERA) project at the Applied Geospatial Research Group (AGRG) where I have the opportunity to work in remote areas in Northern Alberta in seismic restoration assessment research.
